Nikola Tesla

He was a man of extraordinary intellect and imagination, with a mind that could envision the future.

Tesla saw a world united by wireless technology. Wardenclyffe Tower was a step, but challenges ended it. His vision inspires today.
  • Early Years: Born in Smiljan, Austria-Hungary (now Croatia), in 1856. Showed early signs of exceptional intelligence and creativity.
  • Education: Studied engineering at the Technical University of Graz, Austria, but left without a degree.
  • 1882: Discovers the principle of magnetic induction.
  • 1884: Immigrates to the United States.
  • 1888: Develops alternating current (AC) systems.
  • 1893: Demonstrates AC generators at the Chicago World's Fair.
  • 1895: Begins construction of Wardenclyffe Tower, a project envisioned to achieve global wireless communication and power transmission.
  • 1915: Is nominated for the Nobel Prize in Physics but does not win.
  • 1943: Dies in New York City.
